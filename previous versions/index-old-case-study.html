Designing for the V&A

Brief

We were tasked with creating either a responsive website OR native application for the V&A.

They wanted to make visiting the museum much easier and engaging. Allowing visitors to find exhibits that would most interest them, and be able to keep track of the most interesting information, displays, or exhibits that they discover on their trip to the museum.

Competitive Analysis

Through our research into the competitors above we found that they all did similar things and did them well. The one thing that was really lacking from all the competitors was an interactive map.

Being an important feature of the brief it was good to know that there was a gap in the market for the V&A to create a really useful interactive map.

User Research

For this part of the research we started with screener surveys getting an overview of our users. This was followed by guerilla and organised interviews. From our findings we were able to develop the following persona.

With our persona in mind we created a scenario:

Vanessa is planning a hen-do for a friend on the third weekend in June. She looks for interesting events in Time Out. She finds an exhibition at the V&A: Pink Floyd….

With our scenario and user in mind we continued onto our design phase.
