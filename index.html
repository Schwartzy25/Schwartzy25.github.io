Work

The Client

Victoria and Albert Museum (V&A)

The Problem

The V&A wanted to make visiting the museum much easier and engaging, and allow visitors to find exhibits that would most interest them, and be able to keep track of the most interesting information, displays, or exhibits that they discover on their trip to the museum. They would like to feature new exhibits and tips to visitors on what they should explore based on the visitors profile.

The Goal

To create either a responsive website OR native application that would include an interactive mobile map of the museum with the ability to mark exhibits/areas they would like to visit. A personal “passport” or interactive scavenger hunt to encourage discovery within the museum. A feature that allows the user to learn more information about the topic of a certain exhibit or display that they come across and the ability to tie-in with their website to save visits or plan future visits.

My Role

As part of a team of UX designers we took on all aspects of the design process together. Each focusing on specific sections within each step of the process.

Our Process

Discovery

As a group we created a screener survey to find users to interview. After collating the results from these we carried out more in-depth user interviews, as well as a few guerrilla style interviews at the V&A.

A competitor analysis was carried out to see where the Victoria and Albert sat in the market.

Our findings from both the users and competitors is that there is a definite gap in the market of an app that would help users navigate the museum. This was highlighted by some of the quotes we compiled after our user research.

User Personas

Based on all our user research we were able to create a persona and scenario based on the shared qualities of the users we interviewed separately.

Vanessa is planning a hen-do for a friend on the third weekend in June. She looks for interesting events in Time Out. She finds an exhibition at the V&A: Pink Floyd….

Having our persona, Vanessa, and the hen party scenario to focus on during the ideation phase meant we were able to create much more targeted and relevant solutions.

Our Findings and Iterations

  • I’m Lost - Didn’t work due to it’s negative connotations as well as one of the quotes a user gave us “can you really get lost in a museum?” This developed into Guide Me and finally Maps based on more user testing. We found that simplicity was key in helping our users to locate functions that they needed quickly.
  • App Navigation - We tried various different ways to navigate around the app, eventually settling on a bottom tab bar. This went through a couple of variations as we upped the fidelity as shown below.
  • Maps - We had originally tested our idea with two separate maps (one for the overall museum and the other for specific exhibitions) but realised through testing that it would be better for our users to have one unified map.
  • Progress Checker - This was our idea that was most instantly recognised by users so we wanted to keep it close to our original idea. The only amendments were to remove the hint and add a point to show where the user was.
  • Meeting Point - The final idea was specifically based around Vanessa’s specific scenario, visiting in a big group, and during our design phase became part of a process that was too linear. As shown below we amended this so a meeting point could be set at any time from the main Maps menu. Our users appreciated the text functionality so that friends without the app could also receive the information.
